1 · Where we are → where this takes us

Today BWM has almost no brand presence on social. That's not a gap — it's the cheapest growth lever we own.

We already built the hard part — a system that turns interested owners into paying clients. The problem is the one our own audit found: almost nobody is walking up to it. Social is how we get in front of enough owners, every day, for almost nothing.

Right now our channels are empty. That means every post is upside, the brand rails are already locked, and the lead machine that catches inbound is already running. We're not starting from zero — we're switching on a channel into a system that's finished.

We're not building a flywheel — we're switching one on. The catch-and-convert half already runs.

2 · How we win on each channel

Each channel was researched for what actually grows it in 2026 — then bent to our voice and our receipts. (Full cited studies live in the Brain; highlights below.)

One job per channel — so the system reads as deliberate, not scattered.
LinkedIn · the engine room
2026 mechanic → expert-led proof beats brand polish

In 2026 LinkedIn rewards people-powered, proof-first content over company-page gloss. Document carousels and short diagnostic videos travel; native posts beat outbound links (we drop the link in the first comment).

Our move: lead with receipts — what our system did, told in the team voice, no founder face. Diagnostic "leak map" posts an owner can grasp in 20 seconds, then save or send to a partner. Social selling happens in the DMs.

Instagram · the face
2026 mechanic → Reels carry reach, carousels carry saves

Reach is Reels-first; carousels earn saves; the DM is the conversion surface (keyword auto-replies are allowed inside the messaging window).

Our move: R018e "Lo-Fi Cool" footage of the owner's real day — the 9pm follow-up, the missed call — with a tight verdict overlay. Emotion stops the scroll; the receipt earns the visit. "Link in bio" + DM keyword to start a lead.

Facebook · the neighborhood
2026 mechanic → original Reels + Groups, not brand awareness

Meta is paying out reach for original Reels and penalizing low-effort reposts; owners still live in local + trade Groups. Messenger keyword flows ("comment MAP") open a real conversation.

Our move: owner-relief content and unedited "first 5 minutes after a lead" clips. The comment keyword opens a DM our listener qualifies automatically.

Twitter / X · the megaphone
2026 mechanic → threads + reply-game = cheap distribution

Threads and active replies are the cheapest reach for an operator audience. Build-in-public earns trust fast; DMs are gated by the paid API tier.

Our move: turn each hero receipt into a thread; work the replies under bigger service/operator accounts. CTA only after the proof tweets.

6 · How it runs itself — every day

Built on the machinery we already own. Full-auto publish — and if a post is off-brand, the system rewrites it and re-checks until it's clean. Only then does it go live.

Top: the daily post + learning loop. Bottom: the DM lead listener — both reuse systems we already run.
Every day

Post, gate, fix, publish

A scheduled job reads the month's plan and generates each post. It runs through the brand gates — never-price-naked, vendor names, reading level, the image rules. If anything is off, the system rewrites that post and checks again, and keeps going until it passes. Only clean posts publish — nothing off-brand ships, and no slot silently goes dark.

Every month

Re-plan from the data

A monthly job reviews what won, refreshes the channel research, and regenerates the next 30-day plan — retiring the losers, scaling the winners. The plan rewrites itself.

7 · How it gets smarter on its own

The same self-learning engine that already tunes our homepage — pointed at social.

Every post is a small test. The system keeps what wins and quietly drops what doesn't.

Each post is treated as a tiny experiment with a few "arms" — the hook, the format, the time, the CTA, the visual angle. A collector pulls the real numbers (reach, saves, comments, DMs, link clicks) back in. Every night the engine shifts more weight toward what's working and less toward what isn't, then feeds that into tomorrow's posts. Nobody has to read a dashboard for it to improve — it tunes itself, the same way our homepage experiment already does.

8 · It listens for leads in the DMs

A real inquiry in any app's messages gets caught, qualified, and answered — automatically, through the lead system we already run.

We automate as far as each app allows — and always catch and route the lead, even where auto-reply is limited.
What happens

When someone messages us on LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, or X, the system reads it, runs it through our existing qualified-lead judge, and decides: real lead or noise.

A real lead lands in the CRM and pings us on Telegram. The system replies in our voice with a booking link — gated so it can't say anything off-brand. Noise is filtered out.

The honest part

Each app allows a different amount of automation (see the bars above). We reuse the exact lead-judge that already scores our web leads — so a DM lead and a form lead are held to the same bar, with the same record of who was contacted.
