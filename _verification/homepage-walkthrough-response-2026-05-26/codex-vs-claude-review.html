Diagnosis · the gate counts pages, not pixels

visual-qa-summary.md reported all 24 captures passing with overflow 0px and failures: 0. metrics.json confirms the lower-page elements exist in the DOM — proof-cta at absTop: 18885, home-final at absTop: 22023 — but a manual walk of the full-page PNG in slices y≈16,400 to y≈23,200 shows solid white where Category-of-One, Proof receipts, Attribution Honesty, Objections, and FAQ should be rendering on dark canvas.

Two plausible causes: (a) the .reveal IntersectionObserver pattern blocks static screenshot capture so those sections never become visible to crawlers either — bad for AI-search citation, which the page sells as a primary mechanism — or (b) there is a real CSS regression making a stretch of .elev-classed sections render with a white background. Either way the QA gate's pass/fail criteria don't catch it, because the elements measure as on-page even when they paint white.
