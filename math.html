How the math works out

Break-even is one closed job, for most of the service businesses we install.

Back-of-envelope, spouse-readable. Take your average job value. Multiply by your current close rate. Divide the first quarter total by that. That’s how many net new qualified leads the operations layer has to bring in for the install to pay for itself.

Numbers here are benchmark — sourced from first-cohort retention + close-rate averages. Your number is whatever your current operation actually does. We capture that as the baseline before any growth claim ships. This is the Confidence Floor in practice: we’d rather show conservative math than aspirational math.

How to read the numbers

Four evidence classes. Every claim carries one.

ClassWhat it meansExample
measured Live number pulled from our operations layer or a signed contract. Reproducible in minutes. Pre-ship checklist score. Ascend price. Connector audit status.
attributed Revenue or leads tied to a specific campaign via UTM + first-touch / last-touch attribution in our event stream. “Meta campaign X produced N qualified leads in week Y.”
modeled Projection based on captured baseline + known inputs. Always clearly labeled as a projection. “At current rate, quarterly pipeline would hit $X.”
benchmark Industry-level or first-cohort average. Useful for “break-even” math before your baseline is captured. Close-rate ranges. Cost-per-lead ranges by vertical.

If a number on this site is not one of these four classes, it’s a typo. The site-wide closer band at the bottom of every page is the policy: every claim is verifiable. Ask your current agency to show you theirs.
